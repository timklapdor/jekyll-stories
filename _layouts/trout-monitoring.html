NSW trout fisheries are being challenged by:

  1. environmental impacts,
  2. climate change,
  3. pest species,
  4. access restrictions,
  5. habitat degradation and more.

As a result NSW DPI Fisheries has developed a state-wide trout strategy to determine how to best address these challenges facing the fishery.

A key component of the trout strategy is improved monitoring of trout fisheries.

This monitoring will answer questions like...

Are summer temperatures adversely impacting our river trout fisheries?

Is there a decline in trout populations and natural recruitment?

What is the abundance and size of fish?

What are the long term trends in the status of our fisheries?

Is stocking successful?

We held a strategy workshop and stakeholders were asked to indicate key streams for spawning, monitoring and habitat rehabilitation.

These 45 sites will be sampled annually across NSW to monitor trut populations
